## Category 1: Vasopressors and ICU Drugs — MISSING

Your list has no vasopressors. These appear on NCLEX in critical care and shock scenarios, which are high-yield NGN case study topics.

**Proposed additions:**
| Drug | Why it belongs |
|---|---|
| dopamine | Dose-dependent effects (renal/cardiac/vasopressor); classic NCLEX question on which dose does what |
| norepinephrine | First-line vasopressor for septic shock; vasoconstriction and cardiac effects |
| dobutamine | Inotrope; cardiogenic shock; distinct from dopamine; tachycardia side effect |
| vasopressin | Second vasopressor in sepsis; diabetes insipidus treatment; water retention |
| epinephrine | You have this under respiratory (anaphylaxis) — should also appear here as vasopressor/cardiac arrest drug |
| phenylephrine | Pure alpha; used in OR for hypotension; reflex bradycardia |
| milrinone | PDE3 inhibitor; cardiogenic shock; hypotension risk; thrombocytopenia |

**Prof. Linda's response:**
All seven belong. Dopamine in particular is extremely high-yield because of its dose-dependent mechanism. Epinephrine should stay under respiratory AND appear here as a vasopressor.
